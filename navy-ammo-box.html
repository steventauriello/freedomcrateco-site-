History of the United States Navy

The United States Navy traces its official founding to October 13, 1775, when the Continental Congress authorized the creation of a naval force during the American Revolutionary War.

From the Continental Navy to today’s global fleet, the Navy has played a major role in protecting American interests at sea, supporting amphibious operations, projecting power around the world, and defending freedom of navigation across the oceans.
