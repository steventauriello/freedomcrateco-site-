History of the United States Air Force

The United States Air Force became a separate branch of the U.S. military on September 18, 1947, after the National Security Act reorganized America’s military structure following World War II.

Before becoming its own branch, American military aviation operated under the U.S. Army. Today, the Air Force is known for air superiority, global mobility, intelligence, surveillance, reconnaissance, cyber operations, and strategic deterrence.
