History of the United States Marine Corps

The United States Marine Corps was founded on November 10, 1775, when the Second Continental Congress authorized the creation of two battalions of Marines during the American Revolutionary War.

Known for amphibious warfare, expeditionary operations, and rapid-response readiness, the Marine Corps has served in nearly every major American conflict, from the Revolutionary War to World War II, Korea, Vietnam, Iraq, Afghanistan, and modern global operations.
